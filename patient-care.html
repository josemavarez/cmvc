Patient Care Technician Training Program

If you are a compassionate person who loves helping others, consider becoming a patient care technician! Patient care technicians are responsible for caring for and monitoring others who cannot care for themselves. During a hospital stay or medical visit, PCTs are the support personnel to a patient; they assist patients in walking, dressing, bathing, feeding, cleaning, and more. PCTs work under the supervision of healthcare professionals.

Patient care technicians understand their patient's concerns by being empathetic and attentive. PCTs are informed of client cases. They must effectively convey issues, concerns, and updates, to colleagues, and supervisors. PCTs main responsibility is patient comfort.

To become a Patient Care Tech, professional training is required! If you are near Florida, explore the CompuMed Vocational patient care technician training program.

Program
Overview

To show potential employers you have the skills and experience to be employed, you will need to earn a Patient Care Technician Diploma. At CompuMed Vocational Careers, a patient care technician diploma is achievable by enrolling in the Online Patient Care Tech Training program or in person at our Kendall-Miami campus.

CMVC’s patient care technician job training program is 45 weeks long, with 915 clock hours.

PATIENT CARE

What You Will
Learn at CMVC

At the CMVC Patient Care Tech program, students are trained to prepare for entry-level positions as Patient Care Technicians / Home Health Aides / or Phlebotomists. Students are taught the theory, skills, and importance of patient care assistance. CMVC’s patient care technician courses include Nursing Procedures, Medical Terminology, Phlebotomy, Anatomy, Physiology, Home Health Aide, and more. All of the courses provided in the CMVC’s patient care technical training align with the expectations and duties of a PCT.

Patient Care Technician
Job Description

The main concern of a PCT is to keep patients comfortable. Experienced instructors at the CMVC’s patient care technician training will teach students how to fulfill the duties of a patient care technician job.

Patient Care Technician Job Outlook

A significant portion of the U.S. population is reaching old age. Age is beyond control and will continue to climb year after year. The elderly population may need patient care technicians.

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, "Overall employment in healthcare occupations is projected to grow 13 percent from 2021 to 2031, much faster than the average for all occupations; this increase is expected to result in about 2 million new jobs over the decade."

While the BLS doesn't have a specific listing for PCTs, ” Its reported a median annual salary of $30,310 for nursing assistants in 2021.” This number can vary based on location, patient care setting, and experience level.

Program Learning Outcomes

Upon completion of the Patient Care Technician Education at CMVC, students will achieve the following outcomes:

- Provide primary patient care
- Acquire, distribute and administer patient care supplies
- Perform safety checks and ensure cleanliness in patients' rooms
- Accommodate the unique needs of patients accordingly
- Obtain EKG readings and monitor vital signs
- Perform phlebotomy procedures
- Provide physical and emotional support to patient and families coping with loss.

Frequently Asked Questions

(and Answers)

- What are the duties of a patient care technician?
A patient care technician's primary goal is always to ensure that their patients are comfortable and safe at all times.

- Where does a patient care technician work in a hospital?
A patient care technician works in the hospital on the floor. They work directly with patients, helping them with their daily needs and activities. They are responsible for providing support to patients and their families. For example, they may provide assistance with bathing and grooming, dressing, feeding, and performing activities of daily living.

- How much does a patient care tech make?
A patient care technician's salary can vary based on their location and experience.

- What is the difference between a medical assistant and a PCT?
The difference between a medical assistant and a Patient Care Technician is that the PCT role is more focused on direct patient care, whereas the MA role is more focused on administrative duties. A PCT will typically work alongside other nurses and doctors to help provide direct care to patients, while an MA will focus more on administrative duties like scheduling appointments, managing files and paperwork, and other clerical tasks. MA’s are also more in the doctors’ offices.

- How to become a patient care tech?
Patient care technician programs are designed to prepare students for employment in the medical field, with a focus on patient care. To qualify for these programs, students must have a high school diploma or equivalent certificate. Some employers may require additional education or experience as well; however, this varies widely depending on the type of job you want to pursue after graduation.

- How do I become a patient care tech at a hospital?
Becoming a patient care technician is the first step in your career as a healthcare professional. With our program, you'll gain the skills and knowledge needed to help provide care to patients of all ages. You'll learn how to treat patients in a way that helps them feel comfortable, calm, and at ease. You'll also learn how to provide them with information about their treatment plan and answer any questions they may have.
