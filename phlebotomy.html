Phlebotomy Technician Program Information

If you find the medical industry intriguing, consider becoming a phlebotomy technician! Phlebotomy technicians are healthcare workers in general wellness. Phlebotomy involves the drawing of blood and its collection and study of other specimens. Phlebotomy technicians collect blood for blood samples, donations, and transfusions. Once blood samples are collected, phlebotomy technicians label them and send them to the health science lab. Phlebotomy technicians are limited to taking blood from patients and collecting non-blood specimens to be analyzed.

Phlebotomy technicians are members of the medical industry. The samples collected are used to make important decisions about patients' lives and health, such as effective treatment plans. Most PTs work in hospitals and clinics. PT students must enroll and complete the required education to operate as phlebotomist technicians effectively. One can become a phlebotomy technician in Florida with Compu-Med’s phlebotomy technician program at their Miami, FL, campus.

Phlebotomy Tech
Program Overview

Phlebotomy technician schooling aims to prepare students to become phlebotomy technicians! The CMVC phlebotomy technician program takes approximately eight weeks, with 165 hours to complete. The phlebotomy technician program will focus on clinical skills and professional skills needed for the job.

The CMVC phlebotomy technician program offers hands-on lab and clinical experiences completed on campus. A focus on real-world knowledge and application will be applied to prepare students to draw and collect samples from actual patients. Hands-on practice in completing phlebotomy technician job duties may help students learn.

What You Will Learn in this Program

Field experienced instructors are here to help CMVC phlebotomy technician students from the first day! A team of instructors will provide students with meaningful learning experiences that address learners' needs. Collaborating between educators, trainers, and students is vital for growth.Professors will assist students by teaching, coaching, supporting, and demonstrating program knowledge and technical skills necessary for entry-level phlebotomy positions. Field experienced instructors will focus on related phlebotomy topics such as Introduction to Phlebotomy Technician training, Phlebotomy/Law and Ethics, Anatomy and Physiology, Phlebotomy Equipment, Safety in Phlebotomy HIV/AIDS, Phlebotomy Techniques, and Specimen Considerations & Special Procedures.

A commitment to the CMVC phlebotomy technician program is needed to fulfill proper training, education, and certification exam requirements. CMVC's phlebotomy technicians program is dedicated to helping students maintain medical records, learn medical terminology, medical billing and coding, patient care and customer service, patient scheduling, and administrative skills. Contact CMVC today to inquire about the skills and knowledge needed to obtain a phlebotomy technician job.

At Compu-Med Vocational Careers, the college admissions process is simple. First, we encourage prospective phlebotomy technician students to visit our main Florida campus, where the phlebotomy technician program takes place!

By visiting the main campus, students can quickly learn about the phlebotomy technician program structure by getting a glimpse of the program material and requirements.

Previous academic experience is analyzed in the admissions process, and certain factors play a role in evaluating applicants. At CMVC, we seek applicants who have exhibited the desire to learn and succeed.

Phlebotomy Technician Job Outlook

According to the Bureau of Labor Statistics, “Employment of full-time phlebotomists is projected to grow 10 percent from 2021 to 2031, faster than the average for all occupations. About 21,500 openings for phlebotomists are projected each year, on average, over the decade.

More job opportunities may arise with a certified phlebotomist who has completed a formal training program. Trainedand licensed phlebotomy technicians may be needed at hospitals, blood donor centers, mobile phlebotomy solutions, and diagnostic laboratories.

Program Learning Outcomes

The CMVC phlebotomy technician program uses these learning outcomes to measure student achievement and related competencies:

- Demonstrate knowledge of phlebotomy medical terminology
- Demonstrate knowledge of blood specimens from patients of laboratory analysis
- Obtain comprehensive skills to perform venipunctures thoroughly and safely
- Develop anatomy and physiology competencies
- Acquire specimen hands-on practices
- Have an understanding of the phlebotomy course curriculum and the importance it holds in the medical field.

Frequently Asked Questions

(and Answers)


- Which is the best phlebotomy technician school in Florida?
The program at CompuMed Vocational Careers includes training and techniques on industry equipment, including a hands-on component that ensures students are prepared for the fast-paced world of phlebotomy.

- Is being a phlebotomist hard?
It is an interesting job. The main responsibility of a phlebotomist is to draw blood from patients to perform tests. Phlebotomists must always follow strict safety procedures and are required to wear protective equipment. They must also be very organized and detail oriented. Some people who work as phlebotomists enjoy the job because they get to meet new people every day.

- What is it like being a phlebotomy student?
CompuMed’s Phlebotomy program is an 8 week program where you will learn how to draw blood from patients, as well as how to use equipment such as microscopes and other medical tools. You will also learn about health and sanitation practices, which will help you work safely with patients.

- Is a medical lab technician the same as a phlebotomist?
No, a medical lab technician is not the same as a phlebotomist. Phlebotomists are trained to draw blood from patients and perform other tasks related to blood collection. They can work in both healthcare settings and laboratories, but they're usually not involved in diagnostics or testing the way lab techs are.

- Is phlebotomy a great career to get into?
Phlebotomy may be a good career to get into. It is projected to grow faster than the average for all occupations, based on the Bureau of Labor Statistics.
